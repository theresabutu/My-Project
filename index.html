Third Section

In CSS, a margin is the space around an element's border, while padding is the space between an element's border and the element's content. Put another way, the margin property controls the space outside an element, and the padding property controls the space inside an element.

Margin is said to be the outer space of an element, i.e., the margin is the space outside of the element's border. Padding is said to be the inner space of an element, i.e., the padding is the space inside of the element's border.

Borders can be invisible or they could be a thick colored line.

In summary, border is the strip that goes around your div/element of the content. Padding is the space between the content and the border. Margin is the space outside the border.
